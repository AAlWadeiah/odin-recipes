Maqluba

The Crowd Pleaser



A prepared Maqluba dish showing cooked rice with the eggplants and tomatoes peaking out from the rice shell, and toasted almonds sprinkled on top. (Credit for the recipe.)

What is Maqluba?

Maqluba is a dish commonly made across various Arabic countries like Palestine (where I'm from), Iraq, Jordan and Syria. Each household probably has their own way of preparing this famous dish. But one thing remains true amongst all versions: it's a layered dish using rice, vegetables and meat.

The idea behind layering the vegetables and meat underneath the rice then cooking everything together allows all the flavours to beautifully melt into each other. It's cooked low and slow to give time for the flavours to develop and to also prevent the vegetables from burning, since they're underneath the rice. Before we get into the recipe, let's address some common questions about Maqluba.

The vegetables used in maqluba are eggplant, potato, cauliflower, tomatoes, and onions. Different versions use different combinations of these. I find that eggplants, tomatoes, and onions give so much flavour that makes maqluba delicious (so this is what we will use in my recipe). You can add other vegetables you have on hand. There are no hard and fast rules when it comes to this!

My favourite type of meat to use is bone-in chicken. I find that they're tender and create an amazing stock that is used to cook the rice. You can also use lamb (shoulder or leg), which also turns out delicious but tends to make a fattier end-product.

Ingredients

  • Bone-in skin-on chicken
  • Medium to large onions
  • Tomatoe paste
  • Basmati rice
  • Eggplants
  • Tomatoes
  • Cooking oil
  • Spices, including
    • Cinnamon sticks
    • Green cardamom
    • Bay leaves
    • Peppercorns
    • Cloves
    • Allspice
    • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the eggplant and the tomatoes
  2. Get started on cooking the chicken and creating the stock
  3. After the meat has cooked, remove it and set aside, and strain the stock
  4. Now you can start layering all the vegetables, meat and rice together in the pot
  5. Cook the maqluba on medium high heat for about 7 minutes until it bubbles then let simmer on low for about 50 minutes
  6. Turn off the heat and allow the maqluba to rest for a few minutes
  7. Serve by flipping the pot on a serving platter (where the dish gets its name)
